New Species Discovered

Life on Earth is still surprising us — here are the latest finds

Why does this matter?

Scientists describe roughly 15,000–20,000 new species every year. Each discovery reminds us how much of life on Earth is still unknown — and how urgently we need to protect habitats before species vanish before we even find them. Biodiversity isn't just interesting; it's the foundation of every ecosystem we depend on.

Recent Discoveries — 2025–26

🐸
Amphibian

Glass frog with a visible heart

Hyalinobatrachium yaku

Discovered in the Ecuadorian Amazon, this tiny frog has transparent skin through which you can see its beating heart. It was found in a region threatened by oil extraction.

📍 Ecuador, South America
🌺
Plant

Orchid that only blooms at night

Gastrodia agnicellus

A completely underground orchid from Madagascar that flowers for just a few hours after dark. It has no leaves and gets all its energy from fungi in the soil.

📍 Madagascar
🦈
Fish

Walking shark of Papua New Guinea

Hemiscyllium halmahera

Uses its fins to "walk" along the ocean floor in shallow reef zones. One of four new walking shark species confirmed in recent years, all found in the Indo-Pacific.

📍 Papua New Guinea
🐛
Insect

Tardigrade found in urban moss

Macrobiotus shonaicus

A new tardigrade (water bear) species collected from moss growing on a Japanese pavement. Tardigrades can survive in space, extreme pressure, and radiation.

📍 Japan
🦜
Bird

Wakatobi flowerpecker

Dicaeum kuehni

A brightly coloured bird from the Wakatobi Islands of Indonesia, hiding in plain sight for decades among its relatives. Identified finally through genetic analysis.

📍 Indonesia
🌿
Plant · India

New pitcher plant from Meghalaya

Nepenthes mahasiswae

A carnivorous pitcher plant discovered in the cloud forests of Northeast India. It traps insects in its liquid-filled cups. The Northeast is a global biodiversity hotspot.

📍 Meghalaya, India
