School Action Project

Greywater Reuse in Schools

A practical proposal for DPS Patna — saving water that's already been used once

What is greywater?

The water we're wasting every day

Greywater is wastewater from sinks, handwashing stations, and drinking water fountain overflows — water that doesn't contain sewage. Unlike blackwater (from toilets), greywater is safe to reuse for certain purposes with minimal treatment.

A school with 1,000 students generates an estimated 3,000–5,000 litres of greywater every day just from handwashing stations. Almost all of it goes directly into the drain.

Greywater reuse is already mandated in several Indian states and is encouraged by CBSE's own SEWA environmental guidelines as part of sustainable school practices.

Where greywater comes from in a school

Sources in DPS Patna

🚿

Handwashing stations

Outside classrooms, near canteen, labs — highest volume source

🥤

Water cooler overflow

Coolers often overflow or have drainage that goes to waste

🧹

Mopping water

After corridor and classroom cleaning — lightly soiled only

🧪

Science lab sinks

Rinsing equipment, washing glassware — non-chemical only

Proposed reuse methods

What to do with the greywater

🌱

Garden irrigation

Direct pipe from handwashing stations to school garden beds

🚽

Toilet flushing

Collected in rooftop tank, gravity-fed to toilet cisterns

🧽

Floor cleaning

Reuse collected water for corridor mopping (second use)

🌳

Tree watering

Pipe overflow water to tree base channels in campus

Implementation steps

How to actually set this up

  • 1 Map all greywater sources in school — mark on campus layout, estimate volume per day per source.
  • 2 Present proposal to school authorities with this page as supporting material.
  • 3 Start with the simplest fix: redirect one handwashing station drain to a nearby garden bed using a PVC pipe.
  • 4 Install collection drums under water coolers. Label them and assign students to use this water for plants.
  • 5 Document before and after water usage. Use water meter readings if available.
  • 6 Present findings at school assembly or in the next issue of The Green Gene.

Template letter for school authorities

Draft proposal to submit

To,
The Principal,
Delhi Public School, Patna
Subject: Proposal for Greywater Reuse System — SEWA Environmental Initiative

Respected Ma'am/Sir,

As part of my CBSE SEWA project (The Green Gene environmental newsletter), I have researched and prepared a proposal to implement a simple greywater reuse system on our campus.

Our school generates an estimated 3,000–5,000 litres of greywater daily from handwashing stations and water coolers. Currently, this water is discharged into drains. By redirecting it to garden irrigation and toilet flushing, we can reduce our water consumption significantly at minimal cost.

I would be grateful for an opportunity to present this proposal in detail. I have prepared a full implementation plan including source mapping, material requirements, and estimated savings.

This initiative aligns with CBSE's SEWA environmental guidelines and could serve as a model for other schools in the city.

Thanking you,
