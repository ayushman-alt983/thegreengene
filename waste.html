Zero Waste Classroom

Tracking waste from source to destination — and cutting it at every step

The four types of classroom waste

Segregation starts here

Before we can dispose of waste responsibly, we need four separate bins in every classroom — not just one. Here's what goes where:

🟢 Wet / Biodegradable

Compostable

Food scraps, fruit peels, paper napkins, fallen leaves, flowers

🔵 Dry / Recyclable

Recyclable

Clean paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, metal cans, glass

🟡 Hazardous

Special disposal

Batteries, broken glass, chemical-soiled paper, old electronics

🔴 Reject / Landfill

Cannot be recycled

Soiled plastic wrap, broken pens, mixed-material packaging

Waste journey map

Where does our waste actually go?

Tracking the journey from classroom to final destination — documented for DPS Patna's waste stream.

1

Generated in classroom

Paper scraps, food wrappers, pen caps, water bottles — all mixed in one bin currently.

2

Collected by housekeeping staff

Swept and bagged, usually twice a day — everything mixed together regardless of type.

3

Moved to school dumpster

All waste consolidated at a central point. No segregation at this stage.

4

Collected by municipal truck

Patna Municipal Corporation collects from school — but mixes all waste together in the truck.

5

Seur Dumping Ground, Patna

Most of Patna's waste ends up here — an unsegregated landfill site with no composting or recycling infrastructure.

!

Result: everything buried together

Recyclable paper, compostable food, and hazardous material all end up buried in the same landfill — even though they could have been separated and processed differently at step 1.

Zero waste action checklist

Steps to become a model classroom

Tick each step as your class completes it. Progress is tracked below.

  • Set up 4 separate bins with clear labels in the classroom
  • Brief classmates on which waste goes where (5-min session)
  • Assign a weekly "waste monitor" from the class on rotation
  • Replace single-use plastic water bottles with refillable ones
  • Start using both sides of paper before discarding
  • Set up a paper collection drive — donate to kabadiwala weekly
  • Compost wet waste — connect with school garden team
  • Document waste reduction with weekly weight measurements
  • Present progress to school authorities with data
  • Share findings in The Green Gene reader's column
