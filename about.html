The Newsletter

What is The Green Gene?

The Green Gene is a quarterly environmental newsletter published by a Class X student from DPS Patna. Each issue covers one sustainability theme — with current news, actionable eco tips, an interactive quiz, and contributions from classmates (the "fellow Genes").

The goal is simple: make environmental awareness actually interesting and relevant for students our age, especially in a city like Patna where we live with both monsoon floods and summer water scarcity every single year.

CBSE SEWA

What is SEWA?

SEWA stands for Social Empowerment through Work Education and Action. It is a compulsory part of the CBSE curriculum for Classes IX–XII under the Health and Physical Education (HPE) strand.

SEWA aims to connect students to their communities and environment through real, hands-on projects — moving learning beyond the classroom walls.

The Green Gene was chosen as a SEWA activity under the Environment Conscious Citizens strand (Project 6, Section 6.2.2.2), which specifically encourages students to create sustainability publications for their school community.

Goals

What this project aims to do

  • Raise environmental awareness among students in a format they'll actually read.
  • Create a platform for classmates to share eco observations and ideas.
  • Document real sustainability issues relevant to Patna and Bihar.
  • Build a habit of researching, writing, and publishing — skills beyond just SEWA.
